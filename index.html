Sabyasachi Ghosh

I am a computational mechanics researcher and Ph.D. candidate at the Indian Institute of Engineering Science and Technology (IIEST), Shibpur. My research primarily focuses on advanced finite element methods (FEM) and plate and shell dynamics, backed by extensive hands-on experience with industry-standard simulation tools like ABAQUS and ANSYS. To drive efficiency in my research, I leverage my programming skills in Python and Fortran to automate complex FEA workflows and develop robust surrogate models. My research interests includes Finite element analysis, surrogate modelling, physics-informed neural networks (PINNs), sensitivity analysis using Sobol indices, and uncertainty quantification. I regularly translate complex FEM structural vibration data into clear, impactful visual narratives using Matplotlib, Origin Pro, and LaTeX for high-quality manuscript preparation.

Looking forward, my goal is to pioneer research at the intersection of AI and structural dynamics. I am particularly focused on integrating deep learning frameworks like PyTorch with traditional FEA to enable real-time surrogate modeling, structural health monitoring, and physics-constrained predictive simulations. Ultimately, I aim to bridge the gap between computational mechanics and data-driven modelling, contributing to high-impact publications, developing open-source tools on GitHub, and providing technical leadership to solve the next generation of complex engineering challenges.

Research Works
Free vibration and global sensitivity analysis of perforated composite skew shells on elastic foundations
Sabyasachi Ghosh, Salil Haldar
Computers and Structures, 2025

This paper deals with the development of a finite element model for free vibration analysis of laminated composite skew shells resting on a two-parameter elastic foundation and containing cut-outs A sensitivity analysis of the skew shell parameters with cut-outs and elastic foundations using a Gaussian process-based surrogate modelling technique has been proposed. New results of free vibration analysis of skew shells with cut-outs are presented, and the combined effects of skew geometry, cut-outs, and elastic foundation on the frequency response of curved shells are discussed. The proposed methodology provides a computationally efficient and accurate framework for the dynamic analysis of complex laminated skew shell structures.

Kriging uncertainty analysis of skew laminated composite plates resting on elastic foundation
Sabyasachi Ghosh, Salil Haldar
Int J Mech Mater Des, 2025

This study attempts to bridge the significant gap in literature by investigating the combined influence of cutouts, elastic foundation and attached mass, along with thickness ratios and skew angles, on the free vibration characteristics of LCSP (laminated composite skew plates) and investigating the effects of individual composite plate parameters on the frequency response of the plates by considering a Sobol sensitivity analysis.

Dynamic analysis of rectangular cut‚Äëout plates resting on elastic foundation
Sabyasachi Ghosh, Subham Pal, Salil Haldar
Int J Mech Mater Des, 2023

A finite element method for the free vibration analysis of isotropic cut-out plates resting on an elastic foundation is proposed. Plate displacements are modelled according to the FSDT and to account for the parabolic strain variation through the thickness, a shear correction factor of 5/6 is used, and the effect of rotary inertia has been included in the formulation. The present formulation is compared with established results obtained using analytical methods, with and without rotary inertia, and the max variation observed is 2.24% without rotary inertia and 0.02% with rotary inertia. Cut-out plates are validated with results obtained using the finite element method, and the max variation observed between established results and present formulation is 1.3%. Establishing the accuracy of the current formulation, new results are obtained for rectangular isotropic plates with various cutout types resting on an elastic foundation of various stiffness parameters.

Cutout effects on the dynamic analysis of laminated composite plates resting on two-parameter elastic foundation
Sabyasachi Ghosh, Salil Haldar
Mechanics Based Design of Structures and Machines, 2023

This paper investigates the free vibration analysis of cutout laminated composite plates resting on an elastic foundation of the Winkler and Pasternak type. An efficient isoparametric 9-noded Lagrangean C0 finite element method is developed based on five degrees of freedom (d.o.f) first-order shear deformation theory (FSDT), which has the benefit of the requirement of only one variable along the thickness to model the plate equations to obtain the numerical results from the formulation.

Free Vibration Analysis of composite plates on Point Elastic Supports using Finite Element Method
Sabyasachi Ghosh, Sudeshna Haldar, Salil Haldar
J. Inst. Eng. India Ser. C, 2022

In this study, the free vibration analysis of isotropic and laminated composite plates on elastic supports is performed. The element stiffness matrix is obtained using the principle of virtual work, and the stiffness of the springs is added to the element stiffness matrix to obtain the overall stiffness matrix of the plate. Different side ratios (a/b), thickness ratios (a/h), fibre angle orientation and boundary conditions have been considered.
